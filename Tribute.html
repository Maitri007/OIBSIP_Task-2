History

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was an Indian Hindu monk and philosopher who played a significant role in introducing Hinduism to the Western world. He was a disciple of Sri Ramakrishna and founded the Ramakrishna Mission to propagate his teachings.

Vivekananda's teachings emphasized the universality of religions and the importance of spiritual unity among people of different faiths. He believed in the potential of every individual to realize their true nature and achieve spiritual liberation.

Vivekananda's famous speech at the Parliament of World Religions in Chicago in 1893 brought him international recognition and established him as a major spiritual leader. His writings and lectures continue to inspire and influence people all over the world.
